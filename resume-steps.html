Resume & Online Consistency

Your resume is no longer enough alone—it must be a portal to your online presence.

1

ATS-Friendly Formatting

Recruiters use software to scan resumes. Use a single-column layout, standard fonts (Arial/Inter), and avoid complex graphics.

Avoid: Tables, progress bars for skills, and multi-column layouts which often "break" the scanner.
2

Hyperlink Everything

Ensure your LinkedIn URL and Portfolio Link are clickable in the contact section.

View Template: linkedin.com/in/yourname
3

The "Experience Mirror"

The dates, job titles, and project descriptions on your resume MUST match your LinkedIn profile exactly.

Discrepancies in dates or titles are a major red flag for recruiters.

4

Action-Oriented Skills

Map the skills listed in your Portfolio to your resume. Use action verbs like Designed, Developed, Led, or Optimized.
